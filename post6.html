Interest Rates: The One Number That Moves Every Market

Key Takeaways

Set by central banks (the Fed, ECB, HKMA), interest rates are the single most powerful lever in finance, controlling the trajectory of stocks, bonds, property, and savings. When rates rise, stock valuations compress -- growth and tech sectors are hit hardest. Bonds move inversely to rates. Property markets cool as higher mortgage costs reduce the pool of buyers. On the flip side, high-yield savings accounts become more attractive.

What to do now: Favor value stocks over speculative growth. Lock in high-yield savings while rates remain elevated. Consider short-duration bonds to reduce interest rate sensitivity. And keep a close eye on the pivot -- when central banks signal a rate cut cycle, the playbook shifts dramatically.
